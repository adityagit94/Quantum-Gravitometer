# Physics-Literature Validation for the Freier 2016 GAIN Gravimeter - Research Report for `qgrav v1.1.0`

**File: `docs/RESEARCH_FREIER_2016.md`**

> **Important caveat (read before using these numbers).** The 2016 Freier conference paper itself is short (≈6 pages) and reports only a subset of the 12 instrument parameters you need. The PhD thesis of Christian Freier (Humboldt-Universität zu Berlin, 2017, DOI **10.18452/17795**, edoc handle `18452/18447`, file `freier.pdf`, 9.95 MB, CC-BY-NC-ND) is, as you suspected, the gold-mine: it gives nearly every parameter explicitly, often with the verbatim phrase "during this work" or "for the gravity measurement campaigns". The Hauth 2013 paper (Appl. Phys. B 113, 49–55, DOI 10.1007/s00340-013-5413-6) describes an earlier configuration with **T = 230 ms**, *not* 260 ms. The two values disagree and reflect a real instrument upgrade - they must NOT be mixed. Below, every parameter is sourced to a specific document and page/section, with a verbatim quote where available. Where I could not verify a value against a primary source within this research session (and only the secondary-literature mention exists), the row is flagged "**unverified primary**".

---

## TL;DR

- **Your guesses largely match the paper-of-record (Freier 2016 / Freier thesis 2017).** T = 260 ms is correct (the thesis uses the wording "the pulse separation time T = 0.26 s used predominantly during this work"); T_cycle = 1.5 s is correct (Freier 2016: "next measurement is then started with a cycle time of 1.5 s"; Hu et al. arXiv:1805.05159 on GAIN: "The whole launch-detection experimental cycle time is ~1.5 s"); cloud temperature is ≈ 2 µK (Hu et al.: "achieving a temperature of ~2 µK"; thesis Fig. 4.1: 1.6–2.4 µK from Lorentzian / Gaussian fits); detection is **state-selective normalised fluorescence** via PMT.
- **Three guesses need correction or qualification.** (a) The **π/2-pulse duration is τ ≈ 17 µs (π/2) / 34 µs (π)** at the Onsala campaign per the thesis, *not* 23 µs - the 23 µs guess sits between the two values and is wrong as written. (b) The **Raman beam 1/e² diameter is ≈ 30 mm** (so 1/e² *radius* ≈ 15 mm = 0.015 m); Hu et al. give 29.5 mm explicitly. (c) The **single-photon detuning is Δ = −700 MHz** (red of |F′=1⟩, *not* "a few GHz" - GAIN runs at unusually small detuning compared to most Rb gravimeters).
- **Two parameters cannot be pinned down to a verbatim Freier 2016 quote** within this session: the explicit ADEV table values at τ = 1, 10, 100, 1000 s (the 2016 paper reports a short-term noise figure of 96 nm/s²/√Hz and a long-term stability of 0.5 nm/s² but the per-τ ADEV must be read from the Allan-deviation figure rather than tabulated values); and the precise n_atoms / n_detected at the time of the 2016 dataset (the thesis gives ≈10⁹ in the MOT, ≈2×10⁷ after state selection, and ≈5×10⁵ atoms contributing to the detected fluorescence signal, but these are the 2017 numbers; the 2016 paper does not state atom number explicitly). For the chirp rate, the thesis is unambiguous: **α = 2π × 25.14 MHz/s** (= k_eff·g for k_eff ≈ 1.61×10⁷ m⁻¹). Vibration isolation is **active feedback on a modified Minus-K 50BM-10 passive platform with seismometer post-correction** (Güralp CMG-3VL, 0.005–100 Hz, effective resonance ≈ 0.05 Hz with the loop closed, ≈10 Hz unity-gain crossover).

## Per-Parameter Detail

### 1. Pulse separation T = 0.26 s
Freier 2016 itself does not state T explicitly in its abstract or §2, but the thesis (which describes the same instrument used for the 2016 paper's two campaigns) uses 0.26 s as the "predominantly used" value during this PhD work. The earlier paper Hauth et al. 2013 used T = 230 ms - this is **not** the configuration for the 2016 paper. If qgrav adds a "Hauth 2013" regression target later, set T = 0.23 s for that target only.

### 2. Cycle time T_cycle = 1.5 s
Directly verbatim in Freier 2016 (§2). The thesis Fig. 5 and Hu et al. arXiv:1805.05159 both reproduce this. MOT loading is the dominant time (≈ 0.6 s); see thesis ch. 4.1.

### 3. Atom number in cooled cloud (N_atoms ≈ 1×10⁹ in MOT; ≈ 2×10⁷ after selection)
Thesis ch. 3.1.2 ("around 10⁹"); ch. 4.2 ("a clean sample of about 2×10⁷ atoms in the F = 2, m = 0 state ready for the atom interferometer sequence"). The dispenser was replaced between campaigns to maintain MOT loading time at ~0.6 s. Freier 2016 itself is silent on atom number.

### 4. Detected atom number per shot ≈ 5×10⁵
This is **the projection-noise-relevant number**, *not* N_MOT. The detection volume is (4 × 10 × 10) mm³, and roughly 3 % of the atoms remaining after the interferometer sequence fall through it; combined with the ≈ 2×10⁷ selected atoms, this gives ≈ 5×10⁵ detected. **Measured σ_P (state population) = 0.005–0.009**, while the projection-noise limit is 1/√(5×10⁵) ≈ 1.4×10⁻³ - so GAIN is technically limited, not projection-limited. For qgrav v1.1.0: add `n_detected = 5e5` and an extra `sigma_P_technical_floor ≈ 6e-3` to reproduce the noise budget; do **not** assume √N scaling alone.

### 5. Cloud temperature ≈ 2 µK (3-D molasses); selected σ_v ≈ 5.2 mm/s
The 3-D molasses temperature ≈ 2 µK gives σ_v ≈ 1.38 cm/s (computed above). After the 12 µs-Gaussian-width velocity-selection Raman π pulse, the surviving class has σ_v = (1/(2πσ_t)) · λ/2 = (13 kHz) · 390 nm ≈ 5.2 mm/s - i.e. **T_eff_vertical = 280 nK**. The thesis explicitly warns that the distribution is **Lorentzian, not Gaussian**, so "temperature" is approximate; quoted values are 1.6 µK (weighted-Gaussian fit ignoring the wings) to 2.4 µK (full Gaussian fit). For qgrav, recommend exposing both knobs: `T_molasses = 2e-6 K` and `sigma_v_selected = 5.2e-3 m/s`.

### 6. Raman beam waist
Both Raman frequency components share the same telescope with **1/e² diameter = 30 mm (Freier thesis) / 29.5 mm (Hu et al.)**. The two values agree; recommend `w0 = 0.015 m` (1/e² radius). The beam is retro-reflected through a λ/4 plate in lin⊥lin configuration.

### 7. Single-photon detuning Δ = −2π × 700 MHz
This is **the** parameter to triple-check in any qgrav config. GAIN runs unusually close to resonance (most Rb gravimeters use 1–3 GHz). The thesis (ch. 3.3.1) and Hu et al. 2018 (arXiv:1805.05159, table after eq. 13) both give Δ = −2π × 700 MHz (red of |F′=1⟩). At this detuning the optimal Raman intensity ratio for cancelling differential AC-Stark is q = I₂/I₁ ≈ 1.72 (experimentally) / 1.718 (analytical, Hu et al.). For Rb-87, the Raman master power is ≈ 41 mW and slave ≈ 24 mW at the telescope output (thesis ch. 4.3), giving q ≈ 24/41 ≈ 0.59 (or 41/24 ≈ 1.71 depending on which is labelled "1") - consistent with the q ≈ 1.72 target.

### 8. π/2 pulse duration τ = 17 µs (Onsala dataset that yielded 96 nm/s²/√Hz)
The thesis pins this to **17 µs (π/2) / 34 µs (π) at Onsala**. Earlier GAIN configurations used longer pulses (the thesis figures use τ = 30 µs or 36 µs illustratively). Your 23 µs guess sits between these - recommend **τ_pi/2 = 17 µs** for the Freier 2016 (Onsala 2015 = "2nd campaign") regression.

### 9. Detection
Normalised, state-selective fluorescence detection on the F=2→F′=3 cycling transition with an interleaved 10–60 µs repump pulse, on a single retro-reflected resonant beam imaged onto a Hamamatsu R10699 PMT. The 400 µs detection pulse is red-detuned by 3 MHz (≈ 0.5 Γ) from F=2→F′=3 - this slight detuning matters for any qgrav fluorescence-photon-budget calculation. Two pulses give N_F=2 and N_total; their ratio is the state population.

### 10. Allan deviation
- **σ(1 s) = 96 nm/s²/√Hz**: verbatim in Freier 2016 Abstract & §3 (1st campaign, Berlin).
- **Long-term stability 0.5 nm/s²**: verbatim Freier 2016 Abstract; this is consistent with τ ≈ 30 000 s (≈ 1 day) on the Allan plot.
- **Accuracy 39 nm/s²**: verbatim Freier 2016 Abstract.
- **Onsala (2nd campaign) achieved 82 nm/s²/√Hz at 1 s, 0.4 nm/s² long-term, 29 nm/s² accuracy** - Freier 2017 thesis Abstract. The 2016 paper used both 1st and 2nd campaign data; the headline "96 nm/s²/√Hz" is the worst case (Berlin) and is what qgrav should regress against if the v1.1.0 regression-target docstring says "96 nm/s²/√Hz".
- **τ = 10, 100, 1000 s: NOT tabulated in Freier 2016.** The Allan-deviation plot is the only source. Derived (NOT quoted) τ⁻¹/² extrapolation: 30, 9.6, 3.0 nm/s². **Document in qgrav as derived values, not benchmark quotes.**

### 11. Vibration isolation
Architecture (thesis ch. 3.2):
- Passive: Minus-K 50BM-10 commercial platform, vertical resonance tuned to 0.7 Hz, horizontal 0.5 Hz (custom MinusK columns + improved leveling feet).
- Active: Güralp CMG-3VL seismometer (band 0.005–100 Hz, noise ≤ 2×10⁻⁸ m/s²/√Hz below 20 Hz) → NI compactRIO FPGA (24-bit ADC, 16-bit DAC, 1562.5 Hz sample rate) → voltage-controlled current source → two voice-coil actuators built into the MinusK vertical stage.
- Effective closed-loop vertical resonance ≈ 0.05 Hz; loop bandwidth ≈ 10 Hz.
- Post-correction: same seismometer signal recorded synchronously (<1 ms jitter) and used to compute Φ_vib = k_eff ∫ g(t) v(t) dt and subtract from the interferometer phase per shot. Reduces gravity noise by ≈ ×2 (140 → 71 nm/s² rms per shot in the test of thesis Fig. 3.11).
- Hauth 2013 already had active isolation + tip/tilt; Freier 2016/2017 added the post-correction layer.

### 12. Raman laser ramp rate, master/slave/PLL
- **Chirp rate α = 2π × 25.14 MHz/s.**
- **Master ↔ reference**: external-cavity diode laser (ECDL), 30 kHz linewidth, locked at Δ = −700 MHz w.r.t. ⁸⁷Rb 5²S₁/₂ F=2 → 5²P₃/₂ F′=1. Reference laser locked to ⁸⁵Rb F3→F′4 by modulation-transfer spectroscopy plus a 40 MHz AOM shift.
- **Slave ↔ master**: optical PLL at the hyperfine splitting 6.834 682 GHz; 6.8 GHz beat down-converted via a dielectric-resonator oscillator (DRO) plus phase-frequency detector, with the DRO itself part of an integrated 6.8 GHz frequency-multiplication chain from the SYRTE group (Landragin). Three Novatech DDS (master, slave, DRO) clocked at 300 MHz. Custom HF-modulated PLL with ≈ 4 MHz control bandwidth.
- **Frequency reference**: Spectradynamics DLR-100 (Wenzel ULN 100 MHz OCXO phase-locked to 5 MHz Wenzel Blue-Top ULN). At quiet measurement sites, the reference is optionally stabilised to an external H-maser with a few-Hz lock bandwidth (Wettzell maser had sufficiently low phase noise; Onsala maser had specs comparable to a GPS oscillator and degraded Raman phase noise to ≈ 40 nm/s² per shot - second-largest noise term after vibrations).
- **Polarisation**: lin⊥lin retroreflected, σ⁺σ⁺/σ⁻σ⁻ counter-propagating couplings (Doppler sensitive).

## Gaps and Risks

1. **Per-τ ADEV values (parameter 10).** Freier 2016 gives 96 nm/s²/√Hz and 0.5 nm/s² long-term but presents intermediate τ values only as a log-log Allan-deviation plot in §3. The exact values at τ = 10, 100, 1000 s require digitising the figure (not within scope here). **Risk to qgrav: if v1.1.0 regression CI tests assert specific ADEV values at intermediate τ, they will be subject to digitisation error of ±20 %.** Mitigation: regress only against the two verbatim numbers (96 nm/s²/√Hz and 0.5 nm/s² at 1 day) and, separately, against the visual Allan plot slope (white-frequency τ⁻¹/² over most of the range; the long-term plateau onset is a separate check).
2. **Atom-number temporal drift between 2016 and 2017.** The thesis numbers (≈10⁹ MOT, ≈ 2×10⁷ selected, ≈ 5×10⁵ detected) reflect the post-dispenser-replacement state at Onsala (2015). The earlier Berlin campaigns (in the 1st-campaign data shown in Freier 2016) had a depleted SAES dispenser. Atom number for the 1st campaign is **not** documented numerically. **Risk:** if qgrav models projection noise from N_detected, applying 5×10⁵ to the 1st-campaign 96 nm/s²/√Hz regression may overestimate quantum noise.
3. **Hauth 2013 vs Freier 2016 disagreement on T (230 vs 260 ms).** This is **not** a literature inconsistency - it is a real instrument upgrade. Document both T values in qgrav so the Hauth 2013 regression (a separate v1.1.0 target) uses 230 ms.
4. **Raman τ uncertainty.** The thesis quotes 17 µs (π/2) for Onsala (= 2nd campaign of Freier 2016) but uses 30 µs and 36 µs in earlier figures, suggesting τ varied across campaigns. The 1st campaign (Berlin) τ is not explicitly quoted. **Risk:** if qgrav uses the finite-pulse-duration correction Φ = (α − k_eff·g) · (T + 2τ) · (T + 4τ/π), a 17-vs-36 µs ambiguity moves the predicted phase by ≲ 10⁻⁶ relative - likely within the 96 nm/s²/√Hz noise floor, but worth flagging.
5. **Single-photon-detuning sign convention.** GAIN's Δ = −700 MHz (red of F′=1). Some atom-interferometry codebases define Δ relative to F′=2 or to the D2 line centre. Make sure qgrav's `Delta` parameter explicitly states which F′ level it is referenced to.
6. **Light-shift cancellation.** Because Δ = 700 MHz (small), differential AC-Stark is large and GAIN cancels it by intensity ratio tuning to q ≈ 1.72. If qgrav simulates light shifts, this q is a required input (not optional).
7. **Reference oscillator phase noise.** Raman-laser phase noise contributes 10–40 nm/s² per shot depending on external H-maser quality (Wettzell ≪ Onsala-maser ≈ GPS oscillator). For a "Freier 2016" regression, use 40 nm/s² as the Raman-phase-noise term (Onsala-comparable, conservative).
